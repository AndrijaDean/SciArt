Equity
Fairness, inclusion, and justice in how change is shared
Equity refers to justice and fairness in how decisions are made, how benefits and burdens are shared, and how people can participate in shaping change. It includes: procedural equity (fair voice), distributive equity (fair sharing of benefits), recognitional equity (valuing different identities), and capabilities equity (freedom and skills to act).

Sources: EEA, 2024; Langemeyer & Connolly, 2020; Wijsman & Berbés-Blázquez, 2022

Transformation
Deep, system-wide change across personal, practical, and political levels
Transformation means deep, system-wide social change — new ways of thinking, acting, and organising societies. Innovations can trigger transformation at three levels: the personal (values and mindsets), the practical (new actions and technologies), and the political (institutional and structural change).
